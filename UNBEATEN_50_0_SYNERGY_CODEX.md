# UNBEATEN: 50-0 - THE SYNERGY CODEX

## Your Draft Just Got Personal

Picking ten lovely numbers is no longer the whole job. The new synergy system rewards fighters with an actual theme: family bloodlines, famous rivalries, shared eras, stylistic descendants and the sort of boxing history normally explained at 1 a.m. by a bloke guarding the last kebab.

There are **102 synergies in this edition: 27 originals and 75 new connections**. They are useful without becoming a secret "win every fight" button. A clever build gets a nudge. A badly built fighter with two famous names still gets punched in the mouth. Boxing remains boxing, innit.

## What Is A Synergy?

A synergy is a hidden bonus unlocked when two of your drafted attributes come from the correct boxers.

Every attribute remembers its source. If your **Speed** came from Amir Khan and your **Power** came from George Groves, the game recognises the pairing and unlocks **Fast Lane Wembley**. That adds +2 Speed and +2 Power on top of the drafted build.

The important bit is the source, not merely the number. A 95 Speed roll from somebody else does not count as Khan Speed. The stat card is effectively your fighter's boxing DNA, assembled one questionable decision at a time.

## How They Trigger

1. Start a new fighter draft.
2. Spin the legend wheel.
3. Lock a stat from a boxer you want as one half of a connection.
4. Keep drafting until the matching boxer appears for the required second stat.
5. Lock that stat and check **Hidden Synergies**.
6. If the exact source-and-stat pairing matches, the bonus activates immediately.

Example: lock **Chris Eubank Jr Stamina**, then later lock **Conor Benn Power**. The result is **Next Generation Bad Blood**, granting +2 Stamina and +2 Power. It is family history converted into cardio and spite.

Synergies can overlap. One boxer source may help complete more than one connection, so a themed build can become a tidy web rather than a random celebrity buffet.

## Why The Connections Make Sense

The system uses several types of boxing link:

- **Fought each other:** direct rivalries, famous upsets and nights that still start arguments.
- **Shared opponent:** two fighters measured against the same elite test.
- **Same era or division:** boxers who shaped the same competitive scene.
- **Family or national lineage:** bloodlines, domestic scenes and inherited expectations.
- **Style lineage:** speed, pressure, defence or ring craft passed between generations.
- **Championship lineage:** fighters connected by belts, tournaments or divisional history.

The famous shorthand examples explain the idea nicely. **Rope-A-Dope Survives** recalls Ali beating Foreman in 1974. **Lewis Dethroned The King** points to Lewis stopping Tyson in 2002. **Fury's Masterclass** remembers Fury upsetting Wladimir Klitschko in 2015. **Holyfield's Iron Chin** evokes the Tyson rivalry and the 1997 bite disqualification, because apparently normal rematches were too easy.

**British Heavyweight Era** is the Price-and-Allen kind of domestic connection: power, heart and the spiritual energy of a leisure-centre weigh-in. **Klitschko Survivors** is best understood as an era-and-style link. Tony Thompson fought Wladimir; Joseph Parker did not, but Parker belongs to the heavyweight lineage that followed the Klitschko era. We are building boxing themes here, not rewriting BoxRec after four pints in Spoons.

## Eight Builds That Explain The System

**The direct-rivalry build:** Mexican Trilogy Echo is the cleanest history lesson. Barrera Ring IQ plus Morales Heart recreates a rivalry built on craft, pride and the refusal to have a quiet evening.

**The upset build:** One Night In Tokyo combines Douglas Heart with Tyson Power. You get the danger of the favourite and the nerve of the man who ruined everybody's accumulator.

**The family build:** Eubank Family Engine uses Chris Eubank Chin and Eubank Jr Stamina. It is not subtle, but neither was the family brand.

**The inherited-style build:** Tszyu Inheritance combines Tim's Accuracy with Kostya's Power. The surname is doing work, but the required stats still make you earn it.

**The shared-scene build:** Domestic Firestorm joins Khan Speed and Brook Accuracy. It rewards players who understand that a connection can be a long-running domestic storyline, not only a completed fight.

**The lineage build:** Plant And Counter pairs Plant Defence with Crawford Accuracy. They are linked through elite modern craft: making opponents miss, then charging rent for the mistake.

**The crossover build:** Money Mac offers a modest Power and Defence bonus. It is useful, loud and financially ambitious, but it will not turn your fighter into an unbeatable pay-per-view spreadsheet.

**The pressure build:** Porter Maul mixes Porter Stamina with Hatton Heart. Expect close-range graft, ugly clinches and the energy of lads on tour discovering the hotel breakfast closes in four minutes.

## How To Hunt Synergies

**Pick a theme before chasing numbers.** Decide whether you want British domestic chaos, southpaw control, family lineage, body pressure or crossover nonsense. A clear plan beats locking the first shiny 98 you see.

**Secure the rarer half first.** If one source appears less often, lock it when offered. The common half can be hunted later with rerolls.

**Use hub clues.** Hidden Synergies updates as your build develops. Treat each activation as confirmation that the sources match; do not rely on vaguely remembering a fight watched through somebody's phone in a pub.

**Build around reusable names.** Khan, Eubank Jr, Benavidez, Josh Taylor and several others appear in multiple connections. Their correct source stats can leave more than one route open.

**Do not wreck the fighter for the checklist.** Completionists will absolutely draft a cursed collection of historical references. That is their burden. For a serious 50-0 run, keep Chin, Recovery, Stamina and Defence respectable while hunting the theme.

Casual players can ignore all of this and spin. Natural discoveries are part of the fun. The game will tell you when your accidental boxing dissertation has become mechanically useful.

## Balance: Helpful, Not An Autopilot

Most bonuses add **+1 to +3 per affected stat**. They sharpen an identity but cannot repair every weakness. A power synergy does not protect a glass chin. A defensive pairing does not provide enough stamina for twelve frantic rounds. Traits, opponent styles, recovery, cuts, judging and plain bad luck still matter.

That means there is no guaranteed 50-0 recipe. Stacking connected names can create a better build, but it cannot remove the risk from career progression. The ideal result is more interesting decisions, not a cheat code wearing vintage trunks.

## What Changed - And What Did Not

The update adds 75 historical and comedic connections to the original 27, producing 102 synergies for this edition. It expands the combinations recognised by the existing draft system.

Your old careers remain valid. Existing stat sources still work. Saves, accounts, records, belts, leaderboards, achievements, cosmetics, Hall of Fame results and career progression do not need to be reset. Nothing has been taken from an old fighter; new combinations simply give future drafts more themed routes.

## TL;DR

- Synergies activate when **specific stats come from specific boxers**.
- The source matters as much as the stat value.
- This edition has **102 total synergies: 27 original and 75 new**.
- Connections come from rivalries, families, eras, opponents, styles and championship history.
- Bonuses are normally **+1 to +3**, so they help without guaranteeing 50-0.
- Plan a theme, lock the rarer source first and keep the fighter balanced.
- Old saves still work. Nothing needs wiping.

Now build something coherent, something cursed, or something that looks like it was selected by five blokes arguing over boxing clips outside a kebab shop. Then take it to 50-0.
